Overview

Welcome to the AD Gene Expression Trajectory Viewer, a web application for exploring how genes change expression with increasing Alzheimer’s Disease pathology in different cell types. It relies on two key pieces of information:

Quantitative Neuropathology

Donor brain tissue sections from the middle temporal gyrus (MTG) are stained for key pathological proteins and cell types of interest to Alzheimer’s disease. We use machine learning to quantify the staining on these images and define a single continuous pseudo-progression score that can order donors along a trajectory from least AD pathology to most AD pathology.

Gene Expression

Single nucleus RNA-sequencing was collected for ~1.7 million cells and used to define 151 sets of cells with distinct gene expression profiles (supertypes). We compare gene expression levels in cells from each supertype with continuous pseudo-progression scores to identify genes changes in AD.

This tool organizes more than 6 million sets of images exploring every combination of gene and cell type at multiple taxonomy levels. We hope you find this tool useful.

Using the tool

The AD Gene Expression Trajectory Viewer is divided into three panels.

  1. The left panel provides an entry point into the viewer at different levels of the taxonomy.

    2. 'All' shows all 6 million+ rows and can be quite slow, but is useful for finding the most significant metrics for a single gene (e.g., In which cell type does APOE change the most with AD progression?)

    Class shows broad patterns across GABAergic neurons, glutamatergic neurons, and non-neurons for each gene and is the fastest to load. This option allows you to ask questions like Which genes show the most significant disease affects late in disease in GABAergic interneurons?

    Subclass shows patterns across intermediate levels of resolution (e.g., Astrocytes) and allows you to either identify genes with the most significant patterns in cell population or find which cell populations are most relevant for a specific gene relatively quickly.

    Supertype provides a more in-depth view of a specific subclass for the 151 highest-resolution supertypes. If Supertype is chosen then an additional dropdown will appear to select a single subclass. This allows you to explore specific subpopulations of cells (e.g., What are the most significant changes in disease in Microglia and which supertypes are affected?)


  2. The top right panel shows the data including genes, cell types, statistics, and links (details below). Most columns include arrows for sorting ascending or descending by that column and a box for filtering by string or numeric range. Click on a row view images in the bottom right panel. The following columns are included in the data table:

    3. Gene
    HGNC gene symbol for the selected gene

    Taxonomy Level
    Either Class, Subclass, or Supertype, as described above.

    Population
    The name of the selected cell type (e.g., GABAergic, SST, or SST_1)

    All, Early, and Late
    Beta coefficient indicating how much a gene changes across all of, early, or late pseudoprogression in a given population. Positive values indicate increases in AD, while negative values indicate decreases.

    Mean Expression
    Average expression level of the selected gene in the selected population in natural log UMIs per 10k plus 1.

    Comparative Viewer
    Link out to the corresponding view in the Comparative Viewer, a web application that enables side by side comparison of gene expression in matched cells for any gene, comparison with essential donor metadata, and quantification of expression differences (using different statistics).

    Pseudoprogression Plot
    Link out to the full-resolution pseudoprogression plot for the selected gene, including supertype resolution plots for every subclass. A subset of the information in these plots are shown in the bottom right panel.


  3. The bottom right panel shows visualizations of gene expression and gene changes with pseudoprogression across different populations. The data in the panel above are derived from the information in these plots. Up to three plots are shown in this section, and for all plots expression levels are scaled to the natural log UMIs per 10k plus 1.

    4. Gene expression bar plots (upper left) show the expression levels of the selected gene across subclasses. Each column is a subclass (labeled), and each dot represents the mean expression of the cells of a supertype in a single donor.

    Gene trajectories by subclass (lower left) show the expression change across disease pseudo-progression for each subclass, divided into three plots by class. In each plot the x-axis shows continuous donor pseudo-progression and each line represents the locally weighted mean expression (LOWESS) of the supertypes within each subclass, color-coded as indicated on the right. Grey lines represent all subclasses not in that class.

    Gene trajectories by supertype (right) show the same information, but for the supertypes of a selected subclass, and is only shown for subclass and supertype taxonomy levels. The same information is shown in both plots, with the left plots color-coded by supertype and the right plots color-coded by whether or not their abundances also change with continuous donor pseudo-progression.
